Coherence Design Summary & Flagged Gaps

**How vertical coherence was engineered (both LPMs):** every strand has exactly four grade-band substrands moving through the same cognitive arc — informal observation (K-2) → simple causal link (3-5) → full mechanistic/formal model (6-8) → critical, comparative, or quantitative application (9-12). This arc is identical across all six strands in both LPMs, making the progression's developmental logic itself an inspectable, repeated pattern rather than an ad hoc sequence per strand.

**How horizontal coherence was engineered (both LPMs):** the `concepts[].emphasis` field makes cross-strand reinforcement machine-checkable. In LPM A, `Gene`/`Allele`/`Population` (Strand 1) are reinforced in both Strand 2 and Strand 3; `Natural Selection`/`Selection Pressure`/`Drift` (Strand 2) are reinforced in Strand 3. In LPM B, `Selection` (Strand 1) is reinforced in both Strand 2 and Strand 3; `Agency`/`Niche Construction` (Strand 2) are reinforced in Strand 3 — and LPM B additionally achieves *between-subject* horizontal coherence via `associatedDomains` tags showing the same concept doing explanatory work in Biology, Social Studies, and Computer Science contexts across the strands.

**Where the DCR/ICR tension surfaces, precisely:** compare LPM A's Strand 2, SubStrand `OE-STRAND-000122` ("Distinguishes 'this trait helped some individuals survive better' from 'organisms grew this trait because they needed it'") against LPM B's Strand 2, SubStrand `OE-STRAND-000223` ("connect a behavior and a selection process without invoking evolutionary foresight"). Both target the same underlying misconception (evolutionary foresight/need-based teleology) — but LPM A can only ever exclude behavior from the explanation (no agency concept exists to include), while LPM B can include behavior *and* still guard against foresight, because `Agency` is explicitly scoped ("without requiring conscious intention") in its own vocabulary entry. Neither LPM asserts the other is wrong; the difference is a direct, traceable consequence of vocabulary scope, exactly as the ConceptBase's pluralism model intends.

**Gaps flagged for your awareness, not hidden:**
- `gradeSchema`/`subjectSchema` fields in the LPM manifests are omitted (they're optional in `conceptbaseManifest`) — grade bands and domains are informal strings pending Phase 3 formalization. Both LPMs would need re-validation once `OpenEvo-4Band`/`OpenEvo-6Band`-style resources exist.
- No `/alignments/` records exist yet linking `BIO-CORE:Natural_Selection` (000102) to `OE-INTERDISCIPLINARY:Selection` (000213) — deliberately, per Phase 2 governance. A curriculum team wanting to compare these two LPMs directly (as Model C did in your reference materials) would need those alignment records built first.
- LPM B's `OE-STRAND-000224` is the only `required: false` sub-strand in either LPM — a deliberate single demonstration of the obligate/optional mechanism; you may want more elective branching depending on how much curricular flexibility you want the pilot to test.
